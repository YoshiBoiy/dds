Home Data Recommendations

Recommendations

Our recommendations turn the data on the previous page into specific changes to the site’s navigation, layout, and interactions. Each one targets a measurable problem we saw in testing and connects to a specific usability attribute.

1. Replace the hero image with Quick and Popular Services. The original homepage used a large image at the top that took up most of the screen but did not help users complete tasks. Important actions like renewing a license or scheduling an appointment were pushed down the page, so users had to scroll or search to find them. This made the site slower and more frustrating to use. Our redesign removes the large image and adds Quick Services and Popular Services at the top of the page. These sections clearly show common actions like license renewal, vehicle registration, and scheduling appointments, making it easy to scan and choose a task. This makes the site more efficient and easier to use— users can find and start tasks right away.

2. Improve the Locations page with search, direct actions, and clearer structure. The original Locations page relied on long lists of links and bullet points that required users to click through multiple pages to find basic information. Users could not directly schedule appointments from this page, and there was no easy way to search for a specific location. This made the process slow and confusing, especially for users trying to quickly find a nearby center. Our redesign replaces the list-based layout with a structured card system for each location. Each card includes key details such as address, hours, services, and a Schedule button, allowing users to directly book an appointment without extra navigation. A search bar is added at the top so users can quickly find locations by city or zip code, and an Interactive Map View is included at the bottom for users who prefer a visual way to explore locations. This makes the page more efficient and easier to use—users can find, compare, and act on locations in one place.

3. Replace bullet lists with structured sections and add key information upfront. The original License/ID page relied heavily on long bullet lists and links, which made it difficult for users to quickly understand what steps to take. Important information such as required documents, pricing, and deadlines was not clearly visible, forcing users to click through multiple pages or search for details. This made the process confusing and increased the chances of users missing important requirements. Our redesign replaces the bullet lists with clearly organized sections and visual boxes for different license types. Each section includes key details such as cost, requirements, and steps to complete the process. A Required Documents section is added directly on the page to help users prepare ahead of time, along with the REAL ID deadline and other important notices. This makes the site more clear and easier to use—users can understand what they need and take action without extra searching.

4. Reorganize Testing/Training into Appointments and create a dedicated Vehicle Services page. The original Testing and Training page grouped many different links and services together using bullet lists, which made it difficult for users to find what they actually needed. Actions like scheduling a road test were mixed in with general information, forcing users to search through multiple links. At the same time, vehicle-related services were not clearly grouped in one place, making tasks like registration or title transfer harder to find. Our redesign separates these functions by moving testing-related actions into a clear Appointments section and creating a dedicated Vehicle Services page. The Vehicle Services page organizes all relevant actions such as registration, title services, and license plates into structured sections with clear labels, pricing, and required documents. This makes the site more organized and easier to navigate—users can quickly find the correct section based on their goal.

5. Replace Forms/Manuals page with Online Services to reduce unnecessary in-person visits. The original Forms and Manuals page focused on lists of documents and links, which did not clearly guide users toward completing tasks. Many users had to figure out on their own which forms to fill out and still ended up coming into the DMV even when the task could be done online. This created confusion and unnecessary in-person visits. Our redesign replaces this page with an Online Services page that highlights actions users can complete from home, such as renewing a license, updating an address, or ordering replacement documents. Each service is presented in a clear card format with a Start Service button, along with pricing and simple steps showing how the process works. This makes the site more efficient and convenient—users are guided directly to complete tasks online instead of searching through forms.

Closing argument

Conclusion

We realized that for a site like DDS, the biggest usability issues aren’t about missing features or visual polish—they’re about how information is organized and how users find their way through it. Participants were generally patient with a plain-looking interface, but they weren’t patient with ambiguity about which section to try first or where a required document was hiding. Our process combined quick quantitative measurements (task success, time, first-click accuracy) with qualitative notes and quotes, which let us point to specific parts of the UI with real evidence instead of opinions.

Next steps for a real production rollout would include A/B testing the new navigation labels on a subset of users, adding analytics to track first-click paths after the redesign, and running an accessibility audit on the stepper and locator components. We’d also want to set up a process so that the checklist modules stay in sync with policy updates as DDS rules change.
